Welcome to the Medic profession!
Attention: Almost the entire 101 was written before the current version of SWG (aka CU, May 2005). As of NGE (November 2005) everything has changed as the old Medic is now something new, and Doctors are no more.

However, I let this thread stay since everything about crafting is still valid, as far as I know of. - May 2006.

You will have to read some parts as a historical journey, a breeze from the days when the fields were green and ...





Starting out as a Novice Medic you certainly will have quite a few questions and it is my intention to answer them to the best of my knowledge. I have now been Master Doctor for quite a while, but I still clearly remember my early days at the Mos Eisley Med Center. Though there were some papers out there I missed a beginners ABC together with something more in-depth. They always were one thing or the other but never something specific.



This article starts out with a brief theory lesson and a Quick Start. Then it continues with in-depth installments, interlined with hints and tips. Some of it is borrowed, some is stolen, but most is from my own experiences. Naturally I owe a lot to various authors on forums and web sites, no one mentioned, no one forgotten.

I would also like to stress that there are several ways to play SWG, feel free to choose your path.

Personally I enjoy the mix of game and knowledge. SWG is a vast game, way beyond many other games around. Once you start to research on a topic you encounter, you will see that one answer only raises a dozen new questions. And remember, knowledge is power, in SWG too. A speed-grinded title is nothing but a hollow trophy to a shallow mind ,-)

This 101 aims at guiding you at the Medic path. Many parts are written in a way that you can skip them at first and look them up later. Some parts you will never ever need to read, depending on how you develop your character.

1. First Theory Lesson

First, we have to discern the three main attributes that can get injured: they are the Health, Action and Mind attributes or HAM for short. A Medic can only heal the two former main physical attributes, while Entertainers and Combat Medics (CM) can heal Mind.

Every character has a HAM-bar showing the main attributes of each pool. The Health bar is red, the Action bar is green, and finally the Mind bar is blue. By targeting a character you can easily see if the pools are damaged or wounded. Targeting another character will show his/her/its HAM-bar.

Second, we have to distinguish between the two kinds of injuries a character may suffer from, Damage and Wounds, and how they are reflected by the HAM-bar.

Damage shows up as white parts in the HAM-bars and it shows the fatigue or exhaustion of the character. Damage soon self-heals but some stim injections from a Medic make them vanish instantly -- and which character does not want that?

Since damage is more of fatigue than serious injuries, you can heal damage anywhere and under most conditions, such as in wild, heavy fights or on the dance floor.

Wounds are inflicted on a character during fights a character has with creatures or other characters. They show up as black parts of the HAM-bar. Wounds are serious injuries and although they also self-heal while staying in hospital or in a camp their healing is very slow.

Since healing wounds takes special care, you need to deal with them in the protective environment of a hospital or in a camp, unless you can afford a medic capable droid that will provide the necessary aid, so that you can heal almost anywhere (inside some large public structures the droid's medical capability is blocked).

Finally, what tools do you have as a Medic? Basically there are two groups of supplies, Small Stimpacks and Wound Medpacks. Small Stimpacks are used to heal damage, both Health and Action. The Wound Medpacks are used to heal wounds, one type of medpack for each type of wounded stat. (Actually, there are six kinds of Wound Medpacks but this will be explained more below.)

Furthermore there exist State Stimpacks, as well as Cure, Enhance and Resist Medpacks. However, only Doctors have the necessary skill to craft and use these complex supplies.

Quite a few characters become Novice Medics simply to get access to the low-level medic Stim A and B (as well as Woundpack A and B), which only require 5 points of Medicine Use. You can use any medical supply with a usability level equal to or less than your Medicine Use points, unless a certain skill also is required.

Health - physical health, healed by medics
Action - physical energy, healed by medics
Mind – mental strength, healed by entertainers
Damages - fatigue and exhaustion injuries
Wounds - deeper, serious injuries
Stimpacks - drugs that heal damage instantly, may be used everywhere
Wound Medpacks - supplies that heal wounds, used in medic environments
Medicine Use - your ability to use medic supplies, the usability level of medical supplies

2. Quick Start

I presume you already have entered the path of a Novice Medic. I also presume that you know some of the most basic actions in the game, such as how to use the Radial menu, etc.

I suggest you follow the quests your beeping helper droid gives you. The helper droid offers help and aid. Each quest gives rewards and bonus experience points (XP) along the way.
Quote:
The quests are (rewards in parenthesis):
  1. Find Med Center (100cr, 60 Medicine XP, fireworks)
  2. Diagnose (100cr, 70 Medicine XP, cloning pass)
  3. Tend Wound (100cr, 70 Medicine XP, travel pass, resources for Stim A)
  4. Craft One Stim A (100cr, 50 Medicine Crafting XP)
  5. Tend 5 Players' Wounds (2000cr, 200 Medicine XP, Vehicle Rental Device)
You open your overlay map with Ctrl+M, find the Medical Center and go there. Once you enter it you are done. You can always ask the droid for help and it will show you some other tips. You can open help screens with Ctrl+H. The following quests you do in the Med Center.You diagnose characters by targeting them and hit the Diagnose icon at the toolbar. That is the icon looking like a book.

Tend a wounded character: There is a Tendwound icon, it looks like a heart with beams over it, there is another icon looking the same, but that one is heal damage. (See below on Tend Wounds.)

Craft a Stim A with the reward you got from the previous quest. See next installment.

Tend five different wounded persons: You do not need to heal them completely, though that is the custom to completely cure one person and then go on to the next one. Find five different persons and tend their wounds.

Each quest gives you some kind of XP. Medics need to gather two kinds of Experience Points (XP), Medical XP and Medical Crafting XP. Healings give you Medical XP (not from healing yourself though), while crafting medical supplies give you Medical Crafting XP.

Tend Wounds and Tend Damage: I really do not recommend to tend someone. With tend wounds and tend damage you give yourself wounds as a side effect and you must be healed by Entertainers. As far as possible, heal others but not tend them, tend others only in emergency situations. However, you need the proper medical supplies to heal, supplies that you can buy or craft yourself.

2.1 Quick Start - Crafting

Initially you have been given a General Crafting Tool, and four draft schematics (blueprints). However, you were not given any resources to craft them, except for that reward with Quest 3.

Find a Bazaar terminal and browse the local market for resources. At this point you need any kind of Organic (meat or vegetable) and any kind of Inorganic (hmmm, not meat nor vegetable). You need 8 units of each to craft the basic medicine items, hence about 100 units of each will do for a start.

With resources in your inventory, open up the General Crafting Tool and chose among the items you have the ability to craft. I suggest Small Stimpack A, since you are still not in need of any Biological Effect Controllers (BEC), nor any Liquid Suspensions (LS). You simply double click the schematic you want to use.

Next screen is mainly divided in three areas: the resources to the left, the slots to fill in the middle, and to the right is some text we will not cover in this quick-start.

Drag or double click the chosen resource to fill the slot for it. When you click Assemble a virtual die is cast, most often some degree of success. Nevertheless, do not get disappointed if a critical failure wastes your resources and you have to start over again.

You may optionally customize its name. Finally the crafting tool will process the material and place the item in your inventory. Your first medical item is crafted and you also have added to your Medical Crafting XP.

Continue healing damage and craft Small Stimpack A's as you need them, you will soon qualify for the next levels of the Medic skill tree.

2.2 Quick Start - Comment

SWG most often affords several ways to do the same task, healing damage is a good example:
  • Use the Radial menu on the target and select the option HealDamage; or
  • Open the inventory and use the Radial menu of a Small Stimpack, hover over Use, a sub-menu show up, and click on Target; or
  • Target a character and enter the command /healdamage in your chat box (you need to have a Stimpack in your inventory, as opposed to in an equipped backpack).
There are still a few more options, but the installment on Customizing Your GUI will cover most of them.

2.3 Quick Start - Tips

Tip 1: Sit while healing, since that will increase the regeneration rate of the Mind pool. Then you may continue somewhat longer, until your Mind is drained.

Tip 2: Quiet the helper droid's beeps and squeaks by storing it. You can always get it back using your Data pad (Ctrl-D).

Tip 3: Folks like friendly Medics that chat a little, and maybe they will tip you. Show gratitude, whatever the amount, since you might meet them again. Never be rude, you can ignore morons the next time ,-)

Tip 4: Healing wounds give you more XP than healing damage. Wounds give 2.5 XP per wound point healed while damage gives 0.25 XP per damage point healed. Try visiting the small medical centers at the "adventure planets" and you will probably find characters with a mix of damage and wounds *.

Tip 5: Try to buy some Woundpacks, A or B, cheaply from other characters at the Med Center or on the Bazaar. Soon you will be the one that crafts these faster than you can use them--to get the Medical Crafting XP--and you will sell them cheaply. Fair prices are about 65 and 300 respectively, unless crafted with quality resources and advanced components.

Tip 6: Disable the Confirm Crafting Actions in the Options->Misc pane (Ctrl+O), to spare yourself some mouse clicks.

Tip 7: Start out crafting some BECs and LSs when you have some spare time, as you will need them at coming crafting levels.

Tip 8: When you qualify for next level, save yourself some money by asking fellow Medics to teach you. Since that saves you a few thousand credits each time it is fair to tip the helping guy 100-500 depending on level. It is only the novice levels that you need to be taught by NPC trainers.

Tip 9: Credits may be problem at this time, therefore tipped credits shall not be taken for granted. A lot of folks lack common decency and maturity. Running delivery missions from the Mission Terminals give an income and are rather safe. You can take two missions at a time, and roughly: equal rewards in the same direction and distance most often end up at the same location.

Tip 10: Most medics and doctors tend to have numerous items to carry around, and soon they hit the inventory limit. Buy yourself a size 50 backpack, that will add to your limit once you equip it. You can only equip one wearable container at a time.

Tip 11: Use your bank depository. You can join the banks of one city. In that city you have more options at the bank's Radial menu. One of the options at the Use tab is Safety Deposit, which is good for 100 items safely stored. Whatever ever happens to you, the items at the bank will never wear out or take damage from anything. At any time you can empty the deposit, leave the bank, and join a bank of another city.

Tip 12: The Profession/Skill screen (Ctrl+S) is a nice place of information on any profession, skill, ability, and schematic.
* Maybe you will have to advertise your presence in the Med Center by yelling a line now and then.

3. Body Constitution

Previously we covered the HAM-bar and the three primary attributes that the HAM-bar shows; Health, Action and Mind. A character's actions, special moves, and taking hits in fight, take a damage toll on one or many of the pools. Smaller damage will naturally regenerate and heavier damage is healed by medics and/or combat medics. When any one of the three pools is completely drained the character becomes incapacitated for a short while and is susceptible to death.

Now we introduce the six secondary attributes behind the primary attributes. They are Constitution, Focus, Quickness, Stamina, Strength, and Willpower. Three of these determine the resistibility against damage that is inflicted on their respective primary attribute from attacks or actions taken. The three others determine the regeneration rate.

The Health pool is supported by Strength and Constitution, the higher Strength the better the resistance to damage. The better the Constitution the better the regeneration rate when Health is damaged.

The Action pool is supported by Quickness and Stamina. Similarly, Quickness determines the resistibility of the Action pool, and Stamina how fast damage self-heals.

The remaining Focus and Willpower relate to the Mind pool, which Entertainers will heal.

At an early stage a Medic cannot craft Wound Medpacks to heal the secondary attributes. However, any kind of Wound Medpack A or B can be used by a Novice Medic, since they only require 5 points of Medical Use. Trade with other characters or buy such Woundpacks at the Bazaar or from vendors.

The four secondary attributes are regularly overlooked by Medics and the characters themselves, maybe because they are not reflected by the HAM-bar. Opening the Character data pad (Ctrl+C) shows one's own status.

Any Medic can use the /diagnose command at the chat box to diagnose the targeted character within range (about 6 meters), and review all nine attributes, and additionally the Battle Fatigue.

3.1 Battle Fatigue (BF)

Over time characters suffering from wounds and engaging in fights also gather Battle Fatigue (BF), or severe exhaustion and depression. Normally BF is no problem to a Medic, but BF over 250 will linearly reduce the power of your heals, as it also decreases the character's self-healing abilities.

The effectiveness of your healing and enhancements:
Quote:
BF 250 500 750 1000
.% 100 .75 .50 ..25
BF is healed by listening or watching the refreshing amusement of entertainers.

Tip 1: Use the /diagnose command to fully diagnose a targeted patient and reveal all wounds, BF included.

Tip 2: It is always a good practice to FIRST visit cantina and THEN go to the doctor. A little weird? Yes!

Tip 3: Regularly open your own Character pad (Ctrl+C) to check your own stats and get the supporting attributes healed if necessary. Smaller wounds self-heal while hanging around in the med center or in a camp for a while.

4. Customizing Your GUI

Since SWG affords a most powerful GUI and gives you plenty of ways to do tasks, you simply have to make use of them to improve on your work. Some tasks are so repetitively done that you really need to make use of shortcuts.

Exactly how you would like to arrange the icons at the toolbar is your own decision, but learn to use it. Later, refurbish it when you need to. Whether you use macros, aliases, icons, F-function keys, or whatever do not matter, as long as you feel comfortable.

SWG offers 6 different toolbars, each of which can be double sized, totaling 6 times 2 times 12 icons, equal to 144 shortcuts. The upper line of icons are simply the F1 to F12 keys, while Shift+F1 to Shift+F12 relates to the second, lower line of icons. You switch between the toolbars, more exactly to toolbar x, with Ctrl+Fx. Choose one of the empty toolbars and dedicate it for medical use.

Stand and sit are commonly used. Open your Action pad (Ctrl+A) and go to the Other tab. Click and drag the stand and sit icons to two locations of the toolbar. Now clicking one of these icons, or simply hit its F-key will cause the chosen action.

Instant tips: Try to place icons that you will regularly use at several toolbars, such as stand and sit, on several toolbars in the same location. Then it is easier to remember their location.

Diagnose

Diagnose is another most often used command in the medic center, it should be anyway. One way is to use the /diagnose command in the chat box. Or, find the Diagnose icon at the Action pad->Other (Ctrl+A). Drag that icon to make a shortcut at the toolbar. Now you can target a character and use that toolbar icon, click it or use its F-key.

Healing damage

Healing damage is also frequently done and its icon is at the Action pad->Other (Ctrl+A). This command will work as long as you have Small Stim's in your inventory.

Wound Medpacks

Wound Medpack shortcuts can be placed at the toolbar as icons. Simply drag the icon from your inventory (or your backpack if you have them there) to the toolbar location of your choice and a shortcut will automagically be created. The medpack is still in your inventory. This trick might seem odd to some, but it works. The pros and cons are:
+ the icon reflects how many uses there are left,
+ you know exactly which medpack the shortcut is wired to,
- you have to repeat this pick&drag maneuver every time a medpack runs out.

Another way is to use the command /healwound, to which you have to add which attribute to heal, for example "/healwound action". However, writing this every time seems cumbersome.

To circumvent that drawback you can make a few macros at the Action's Macro pane. Make each macro such as "/healwound action;", resulting in six one-line macros. You may name them whatever you like. Drag their icons to the toolbar.

Still another way is to create aliases for single or chained commands. The generic syntax is:
Quote:
/alias alias_name /command[;/next_command;...]
For example: "/alias hwa /healwound action;". Now the much shorter alias "/hwa" will heal the action wounds of the target.

However, aliases have to be loaded manually before you can use them. This is easily done using the command "/load aliases.txt". Saving is automatic.

Commands, macros and aliases will work as long as you have appropriate supplies in your inventory. However, you cannot predict which one is to be used if several of the same kind are there.

Does it really matter which medpack that is used? Not really, as long as it does not matter to you. However, what if you have both one Health Woundpack A (that roughly heals 20 wound points at this point of your career) and one Health Woundpack B (that heals for over 60 wound points, nicely crafted)? Do you want to use that better pack with a 10 points wound? As long as you have both in your inventory the "/healwound health" will work, but I don't know which one of the two medpacks SWG will pick. Do you?

Do whatever appeal most to you. You might also have all supplies in a container of their own and have that container open all of the time. Make its window as small as possible, still the supplies visible. Then simply double-click the one you want to apply at the targeted character.

Double-clicking any item will always pick the default option (No. 1) at the Radial menu.

Why not finally add a shortcut to your General Crafting Tool, since you are to use it a lot for your closest future.

Soon you will have more items and abilities to take care of. Hence I predict several rearrangements, many new macros and aliases.

5.1 Food and Chemical Crafting Tool

The General Crafting Tool (GC Tool) suffices to begin with, but soon you need the specialized version: Food and Chemical Crafting Tool (FCC Tool). In spite of having the schematic to craft such a tool yourself, I suggest you do not do that. Unless you are an Artisan, you do not have skill enough to craft a quality tool. Besides, do you have good enough resources? You better head to the Bazaar and buy one.

The higher Tool Effectiveness the better, since that improves the result while crafting. It should be easy to find tools with 14.9+. Max. rating is 15.00. "Yes, they exist!" in spite of any rumors. I have two myself.

The FCC Tool is required with the more complex schematics. You can find a schematic's complexity in the Data pad (Ctrl+D). From zero to 15 a GC Tool will do, from 16 and up an FCC Tool is necessary.

Furthermore, the FCC Tool partly enables you to experiment while crafting. However, you also need to be close to a "crafting station" to fully enable that feature. See later installments on experimentation.

As soon as you can afford two FCC Tools, buy yourself a second one. Why so? Because the higher complexity of the item you want to craft, the longer the processing time. With two tools you simply switch between them.

Do not mix up the FCC Tool with those FCC Stations you will see around. We will come to these in the next chapter, but they simply are not the same animal.

Next stop will be the Cloning Center, to pay the insurance for your new belongings. That way they don't wear out that quickly every time you face death. Never forget to pay your insurance and your life-after-death will be somewhat easier to bear.

NOTE: Sometimes you will get an error message while attempting to craft, something similar to you have to free the output hopper. That is actually from your previous crafting session (which indeed also raised an error message, saying something about your inventory being full and the item is left at the output hopper).

First, make some room in your inventory. Then, bring the tool's Radial menu, hover over Use and at the sub-menu that pops up, click the Empty Output Hopper to free the item.

Finally, there is no maintenance or other cost bound to the crafting tool.

5.2 Food and Chemical Crafting Station

There are typically two kinds of crafting sessions; simple crafting and power crafting sessions.

Simple crafting is crafting for casual items, which you can do at any peaceful place.

A power crafting-session has the goal of crafting more complex or powerful products, or to make manufacture schematics. You need to be close to a public or private crafting station, or a droid (see installment on droids). You never do anything with the station since your specialized tool automatically connects to it.

However, recently a feature was added to crafting stations. Now you can simply double click the station and the appropriate crafting screen shows up. Since this is new some questions are pending.

The crafting station has to be of the same kind as the crafting tool used. FCC Tools connect to FCC Stations, etc.

Initially you will do well with the Public FCC Stations that you easily find within NPC cities. In such cities they are found at public outdoor areas, and often at some guild hall. Very often one is found nearby star ports or medical centers.

The more complex the schematic is, the higher requirement on the FCC Station. Medium complexities require a Public FCC Station, while high complexities require a Private FCC Station. The more complex schematics simply do not show up in your FCC Tool unless it can connect to the appropriate FCC Station.

This is a summary of complexities and minimum requirements:
Quote:
.0 - 15: General Crafting Tool
16 - 20: Specialized Crafting Tool
21 - 25: Specialized Crafting Tool and Public Crafting Station
26 - .∞: Specialized Crafting Tool and Private Crafting Station
NOTE: As stated earlier, power crafting always require a nearby Crafting Station or a droid, no matter the complexity.

Many player cities use to have Private Crafting Stations available, for example at their mall. In spite of their name, Private Crafting Stations are not that private. If you can come close to one, your FCC Tool will connect to it. See paragraph on Station Hopper.
You can also buy yourself a Private FCC Station to place in your house or wherever you have admin. rights. They are sold by Master Architects.

Station Hopper -- The privacy with Private FCC Station is in the Station Hopper. If you connect to a private crafting station located where you also have admin. rights, another feature is enabled at the FCC Tool. Bring its Radial menu -> Use another option is available, the Station Hopper.

The Station Hopper can serve as another storage, with some pros and cons: It is convenient to have resources stashed closely to where you will use them, but since the number of items also counts towards the item limit of the house the advantage of the hopper is limited. (Every house has an upper limit for the number of items in it, depending on the size of the house.)

FCC Stations have a Functionality Rating, the max. limit is 45.00. Since such a rating requires uber high quality resources they are very rare. At some galaxies it might never have been able to build such stations yet, I do not know.

The higher the rating, the better the random result while crafting will be. Though it is not fully agreed upon, many solid reports tend to show that the sum of the station's rating added to the tool's effectiveness, boosts the outcome every tenth step. That is, at ... , 30, 40, 50 and -- the dream -- 60, you will see a minor boost to your crafting in some way or another.

The previous reasoning implies that you should buy the best FCC Tools you can find, and an FCC Station such that the sum mentioned passes 50, until you find that uber FCC Station 45 Golden Be-Bop-De Luxe.

Crafting stations do not need power or maintenance. Once bought it does not cost you anything.

NOTE: If many stations are placed close to each other you better stay really close to the one you want to connect to. They tend to overrule each other in such circumstances.

5.3 Food and Chemical Crafting Factory

If you plan to manufacture larger volumes an FCC Factory is an absolute must-have. Actually, the more complex medical supplies require identical sub-components, that is with identical serial numbers. Such components can only come from factory crates.

Of course you can buy such crates, normally by ordering them from someone running a vendor, since sub-components are not commonly stocked.

FCC Factories are made by Architect wannabees. It comes as a deed (see Appendix B for information on deeds). They are placed like any building and will use one of your ten lots, as any small structure does. Within player cities you need zoning rights given by the city Mayor or the city Militia.

Every kind of factory takes an hourly maintenance fee while placed. The cost is 60 credits per hour, in other words 1440 credits per day. This fee is whether the factory actually idle or is processing. If the maintenance pool is emptied, the cost is deducted from your bank account automatically.

An FCC Factory needs power only while it is running. The power consumption is 50 power units an hour *. If it runs out of power it simply stops.

Neither maintenance nor power may be taken back once fed into the factory.

The cost of re-deed, that is taking the structure down and get the deed back, is always 50 times a structure's hourly maintenance. Hence, an FCC Factory has a re-deed cost of 3000 credits, withdrawn from the factory's maintenance pool. Assure yourself that all figures at the dialogue boxes are green before confirming the re-deed.

Any surplus power and maintenance (except the re-deed cost of 3000 cr) remain in the deed.

Most of the options at the factory's Radial menu are pretty obvious and need not be described in a Medic's 101.

You must have a Manufacture Schematic in your data pad to feed the schematic hopper (see installment on schematics). Unless the scheamtic is consumed you can take the schematic back anytime, for example to run another batch in between.

The serial number is in the manufacture schematic, so that every item manufactured from it get its serial number.

The following paragraphs give the answer to the Number One Question on factories in any crafting forum.

At the factory's input hopper you must also place exactly the same resources that were used while making the Manufacture Schematic. This means, if a resource named "Solohan" was used while making the schematic, you need to have more of that same resource to run a batch. It is not sufficient to have for example Dolovite Iron, but both the NAME and the TYPE need to be correct for the factory to start. The same holds for sub-components, they all need to have identical serial numbers as the one used while making the schematic.

One option at the Radial menu is to get a list of what the factory actually requires with the present schematic. Pay attention to resource names and serial numbers of sub-components.

Factory runtime per item is much slower than manual crafting. You estimate the runtime by 8 seconds each complexity point. And, "Yes, the factory runs even though you are off-line!"

The factory input hopper may be used as another storage. It may hold up to 100 items and containers. However, the input hopper is locked and not available while the factory is running.

The factory output hopper can hold 100 items too, but you cannot add anything to it. Only the factory crates go there, but you cannot put a crate back only you have picked one up.

You may open the output hopper at any time. However, that is not in any way intuitive since there is no visible option for that. You open the output hopper by clicking the Option tab at the Radial menu, taking no notice of the Stop Manufacturing sub-menu that pops up. The factory continues though its output hopper is open.

Instant tip: Since storage is a problem to every crafter, leave the crates at the output hopper until you really need them. Only make room for the next batch, if that is necessary.

You will get a system message if the factory runs out of resources, or when the preset number of items is made. The only in-game mail ever sent from the factory is if maintenance runs out, but it is then automatically deducted from your bank-account.

Double-clicking (Examine by default) your running factory reveals what item is currently manufactured, each item's runtime and how many items made. However, that figure does not show the total number of items made, but how many items made since the most recent start or restart of the factory.

Footnote:
* To complicate the power supply further: If PE of the power resource used is over 500 you get "bonus" power from each resource unit. The formula is:
Quote:
....┌
....│PE ≤ 500 : 1
P = ┤
....│PE > 500 : PE / 500
....└
E.g.: PE is 750, gives P = 1.5, which can be read as "every resource unit is actually considered as one and a half by the consuming apparatus".

Radioactive power seems to be the best choice here, but is initially more expensive if you harvest yourself because of the cost of the fusion generator. Wind power plants are commonly the beginner's choice.

Power may always be bought at the Bazaar for about 2 - 3 cpu (credits per unit). Look for Wind or Solar Liquid Energy, or Radioactive Energy. In spite of its name, Petro Fuel is not used for power.
